### 1. Safety & Health Policy Statement
**[COMPANY NAME]** is committed to providing a safe and healthful workplace for every employee. Safety is a core value, not a competing priority. Management will provide the resources, training, and equipment reasonably necessary to perform work safely, and every employee is expected to follow safe work practices and report hazards. **Every employee has stop-work authority** and is expected to use it when a task appears unsafe, without fear of retaliation.

### 2. Responsibilities
- **Management / Owner:** Set safety expectations, provide resources, review program performance.
- **Supervisors / Foremen:** Enforce safe work practices, conduct pre-task planning and toolbox talks, correct hazards, and document training.
- **Qualified Persons:** Persons who, by training and experience, are able to identify and avoid electrical hazards. Only qualified persons perform work on or near exposed energized parts.
- **Employees:** Follow this program, use required PPE, inspect tools and equipment, report hazards and incidents, and participate in training.

### 3. Hazard Identification & Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)
Before non-routine tasks, the crew completes a Job Hazard Analysis (see the JHA template) to identify task steps, hazards, and controls. Daily pre-task planning is performed on every jobsite. [Customize to describe your JHA process and who approves it.]

### 4. Electrical Safe Work Practices
- **Work de-energized whenever feasible.** Energized work is performed only when de-energizing introduces additional or increased hazards or is infeasible, and only by qualified persons with appropriate controls and an energized work permit.
- Verify de-energization with a properly rated tester ("test before touch").
- Maintain approach distances from exposed energized conductors and parts.
- Use insulated tools rated for the voltage when working on or near energized parts.
- Treat all conductors as energized until tested and verified de-energized and locked out.
- Use GFCI protection for temporary power and on all 120V receptacle circuits used for construction.

### 5. Lockout/Tagout (Energy Control)
Hazardous energy is controlled per the company **Lockout/Tagout Program** and **equipment-specific LOTO procedures**. Authorized employees apply their own locks and tags, verify zero energy, and remove them only when work is complete. See the *LOTO Procedure Template* and *Equipment-Specific LOTO Form*.

### 6. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
PPE is selected based on a documented **PPE Hazard Assessment**. At a minimum, employees use eye protection, appropriate hand protection, foot protection, and head protection as conditions require. Electrical-rated PPE (insulating gloves, arc-rated clothing) is used where an electrical hazard analysis identifies the need. Inspect PPE before each use; remove damaged PPE from service.

### 7. Ladders, Scaffolds & Working at Heights
Follow the company **Ladder Safety Program**, **Scaffold Safety Program**, and **Fall Protection Plan**. Inspect ladders and scaffolds before use; use fall protection where required by the task and conditions.

### 8. Hand & Power Tools
Inspect tools before use, remove damaged tools from service, use guards as designed, and use the correct tool for the task. Cord-and-plug tools are inspected for damage and used with GFCI protection.

### 9. Hazard Communication
Chemicals (solvents, lubricants, sealants, etc.) are managed under the company **Hazard Communication Program**, with an up-to-date chemical inventory, accessible Safety Data Sheets, and proper container labeling. Employees are trained on the chemicals they may be exposed to.

### 10. Training
Employees receive new-hire safety orientation and task-specific training before performing work that requires it. Qualified- and competent-person designations are documented. Refresher training is provided per the training matrix and when performance or conditions indicate a need.

### 11. Incident Reporting & Investigation
All injuries, illnesses, near misses, and property damage are reported to a supervisor as soon as safely possible and documented on the **Incident Report Form**. Incidents are investigated to identify root causes and corrective actions; corrective actions are tracked to completion.

### 12. Emergency Action & First Aid
Each jobsite has emergency contacts, the nearest medical facility identified, and a plan for summoning help. For electrical shock: do not touch the victim if still in contact with the source; de-energize if possible, call emergency services, and provide first aid/CPR if trained. See the **Emergency Action Plan** and **Shock Response Procedure**.

### 13. Recordkeeping
Training records, inspections, JHAs, incident reports, and program reviews are maintained per the company **Safety Records Retention Schedule**.

### 14. Program Review
This manual is reviewed at least annually and updated when work, equipment, personnel, or requirements change.
