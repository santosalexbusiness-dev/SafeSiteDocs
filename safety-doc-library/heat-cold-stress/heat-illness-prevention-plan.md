## Heat Illness Prevention Plan

### 1. Purpose & Scope
This plan describes how **[COMPANY NAME]** protects employees from heat-related illness during work in hot indoor or outdoor environments. It applies to [all field employees / specific crews].

### 2. Responsibilities
- **Management:** Provide water, shade/rest resources, and training; support the plan.
- **Supervisors:** Monitor conditions and employees, enforce water/rest/shade, adjust work as heat rises, and respond to symptoms.
- **Employees:** Drink water regularly, take breaks, watch coworkers, and report symptoms early.

### 3. Water
Provide cool, potable drinking water. Encourage drinking small amounts often (e.g., about one cup every 15–20 minutes). Keep water close to the work area and refill before it runs out. **Target supply:** [specify quantity per employee per shift].

### 4. Rest & Shade
Provide access to shade or a cooler area for rest breaks. Allow and encourage **preventive cool-down rest breaks** when employees feel the need. Increase break frequency as temperature and workload rise. **Shade location(s):** [____].

### 5. Acclimatization
New employees and those returning from time away are at higher risk. Gradually increase workload/exposure over the first several days, and watch them and all employees closely during heat waves. (See the *Heat Acclimatization Plan*.)

### 6. High-Heat Practices (customize triggers)
When conditions reach **[your trigger — e.g., a set temperature/heat index]**, take added steps:
- Increase the frequency of water and rest reminders.
- Maintain effective communication and observation (buddy system / supervisor checks).
- Pre-shift briefing on heat illness and the day's plan.
- Adjust schedules (earlier starts, more breaks) where feasible.

### 7. Recognizing & Responding to Heat Illness
| Condition | Common Signs | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Heat exhaustion | Heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, nausea, headache | Move to shade/cool area, give water, cool the body, rest, monitor; seek medical help if not improving |
| **Heat stroke (emergency)** | Confusion, slurred speech, hot/dry or wet skin, fainting, seizures | **Call 911 immediately.** Move to cool area and cool aggressively (water, ice, fanning) while waiting for help |

Never leave a person with serious symptoms alone. When in doubt, call for emergency help.

### 8. Emergency Procedures
- Emergency number: **911**   Company contact: [NAME / PHONE]
- Know each jobsite's address and nearest medical facility (record on the site plan).
- Means to summon help and provide clear directions to the site.

### 9. Training
Train supervisors and employees on heat hazards, this plan, water/rest/shade, acclimatization, symptom recognition, and emergency response. Document training on the training log.

### 10. Review
Review this plan at least annually and before each hot season, and update it when conditions or requirements change.

**Daily tool:** Use the *Heat Stress Daily Checklist* and *Water/Rest/Shade Checklist* on hot days.
