## Topic: Fall Protection Basics
Falls are one of the most common causes of serious injuries in construction. The good news is they're preventable. The goal today is simple: know when you need fall protection, what to use, and how to use it correctly.

### 5-Minute Lesson
- **Know your fall hazards.** Unprotected edges, holes, roofs, leading edges, and elevated platforms are the usual ones. Identify them before you start, not after.
- **Pick the right protection.** Common options include guardrails, safety nets, and personal fall arrest systems (a properly fitted harness, a lanyard or self-retracting lifeline, and a rated anchor point). Use what the task calls for.
- **Inspect before every use.** Check harnesses, lanyards, and connectors for cuts, frays, burns, corrosion, or damaged hardware. If it's damaged, take it out of service — don't "make it work."
- **Anchor correctly.** Connect to an anchor that can hold the required load, positioned to keep your fall distance short. Mind swing falls.
- **Cover and protect holes.** Floor holes and openings get a secured, marked cover or a guardrail — every time.
- **Have a rescue plan.** If someone falls and is suspended, you need a way to get them down quickly. Know the plan before work starts.

### Key Hazards
- Unprotected edges, openings, and floor/roof holes
- Improperly fitted, damaged, or unanchored fall arrest equipment
- Wrong or overloaded anchor points; swing-fall hazards
- Working near skylights, fragile surfaces, or leading edges

### Discussion Questions
1. Where exactly are the fall hazards on **this** job today?
2. What fall protection are we using at each location — and did everyone inspect their gear?
3. If someone fell and was left hanging in a harness, what's our rescue plan right now?
4. Has anyone had a close call with a fall? What would have helped?

### Crew Checklist
- [ ] Fall hazards identified and pointed out
- [ ] Correct fall protection selected for each task
- [ ] Harnesses/lanyards/connectors inspected and fit checked
- [ ] Anchor points rated, correct, and positioned to limit fall distance
- [ ] Holes and openings covered/marked or guarded
- [ ] Rescue plan known by the crew
- [ ] Everyone knows they can stop work if something isn't safe
